Calculations

Weight of solid, WA = 98000dynes

Angle of Inclination, Θ = 00

Normal Force, N = WA *cos Θ = 98000 dynes

Frictional Force, Ff=µS ✕ N = 392.4dynes

Sliding Force, SF=WA ✕ sin Θ = 0 dynes

At Critical Angle of Inclination Θc, Frictional force = Sliding force

For this experiment, consider critical angle approximately equal to the angle at which the block A just starts to slide

µS ✕ WA ✕ cos Θc = WA ✕ sin Θc

Coefficent of static friction, µS = sin Θc/cos Θc = tan Θc

Aim

To measure the critical angle of inclination for a given surface and to calculate it's coefficient of static friction using inclined plane and block system.

Apparatus
Inclined Plane with adjustable angle

Wooden blocks of different masses

Different types of sliding surfaces with varying co-efficient of friction

Critical Angle of inclination = This is the angle of inclination at which the Frictional force and the Sliding force are equal to each other. When the inclination increases even slightly beyond the critical angle, the body will start sliding down the surface.

Procedure
Select a sliding surface and place it on the adjustable plane, which is kept horizontal at the beginning of the experiment.

Place the wooden block on the sliding surface

Slowly start raising the angle of inclination of the adjustable plane.

Record the angle of inclination at which the block just starts to slide.

Record your observations and calculate the coefficient of static friction for the given surface.

Repeat the procedure for another sliding surface and calculate the coefficients of friction. Verify the results with the given values of friction coefficient.

For a given surface, repeat the experiment by changing the mass of the wooden block and compare with the earlier readings.
